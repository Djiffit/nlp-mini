This move comes barely a month after Qantas suspended a number of services between Australia, Indonesia, Thailand and Malaysia in the wake of the Asian economic crisis. In a joint statement with Tourism Minister Andrew Thomson, it said two new flights would leave Bombay on Monday and Tuesday nights from <TIMEX>March 30</TIMEX>, with the third departing each Thursday from <TIMEX>August 6</TIMEX>. This will add nearly 700 seats a <TIMEX>week</TIMEX> on the route. Thomson, in India to talk to tourism leaders, said the flights would provide extra support to the growing tourism market. Qantas will also appoint a Bombay-based public relations consultant.

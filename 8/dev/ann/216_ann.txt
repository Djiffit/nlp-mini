Poland to participate in preparations for NATO summit



WARSAW, Poland (AP) _

Poland, Hungary and the Czech Republic can now participate in NATO strategy planning, because all NATO member states have approved their joining the alliance, German defense minister Rudolf Scharping said <TIMEX>Saturday</TIMEX>.

The Netherlands <TIMEX>early this week</TIMEX> became the last country to ratify NATO expansion to include the three former Warsaw Pact nations.

The three are expected to join in <TIMEX>April</TIMEX>, as part of marking NATO's 50th anniversary. With their membership secure, they also can participate in planning for the NATO summit in <TIMEX>April</TIMEX>.

Scharping, ending a <TIMEX>two-day</TIMEX>  visit, said Poland was well prepared to join the alliance. Germany has been a strong advocate of Poland's access to NATO, saying it will serve European security and stability.
